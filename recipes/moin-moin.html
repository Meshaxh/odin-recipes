Moin Moin (Moi Moi) Recipe

Ingredients (Makes 6-8 portions):

  • 2 cups peeled black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • 1 large red bell pepper (tatashe)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo) – optional for heat
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (or palm oil, if preferred)
  • 2 teaspoons seasoning powder or bouillon
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional, for flavor)
  • 2 boiled eggs (sliced – optional)
  • 1 can of sardines or mackerel (optional)
  • 2-3 cups water (for blending and texture)

Optional Add-ins:

  • Cooked minced beef or chicken
  • Chopped spinach or ugu
  • Corned beef

Tools

  • Blender
  • Steaming containers (ramekins, foil bowls, or banana leaves)
  • Steamer or large pot with rack

Instructions

  1. Soak and Peel the Beans (if not already peeled):
    • Soak beans in water for about 10 minutes, then rub between your hands to remove the skin. Rinse thoroughly to remove skins.
  2. Blend the Mixture:
    • In a blender, combine beans, bell pepper, scotch bonnet, and onion with water.
    • Blend until very smooth (you may need to do this in batches).
    • The texture should be like pancake batter—smooth but not too runny.
  3. Season and Mix:
    • Pour the mixture into a large bowl.
    • Add oil, seasoning powder, salt, crayfish (if using), and mix well.
    • Gently fold in sliced eggs, fish, or other add-ins
  4. Prepare the Cooking Container:
    • Grease ramekins, small containers, or wrap banana leaves into pockets.
    • Pour the mixture into containers, filling about 3/4 full.
  5. Steam the Moin Moin:
    • Place in a steamer or pot with a rack and a bit of boiling water at the bottom.
    • Cover the pot tightly (you can use foil or a plastic bag over the top to trap steam).
    • Steam on medium heat for 45–60 minutes, or until firm and cooked through.
  6. Check for Doneness:
    • Insert a toothpick—it should come out clean.
    • Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Serving suggestions

  • With jollof rice, fried rice, or pap (ogi)
  • As a light meal on its own
  • With garri soakings for a traditional combo
