Fried-Rice

Here's a classic Nigerian fried rice recipe — vibrant, flavorful, and often served at parties or festive gatherings.

Nigerian Fried Rice Recipe

Ingredients (Serves 4–6):

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 ½ cups chicken or beef stock (or water + seasoning)
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup diced green beans
  • 1/2 cup sweet corn (optional)
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/2 cup diced red & green bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup diced liver or shredded cooked chicken (optional)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 2–3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • 1–2 seasoning cubes or bouillon
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps:

  1. Parboil the Rice:

    • Rinse and parboil the rice for 8–10 minutes.
    • Drain, rinse with cold water, and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables

    • Dice all veggies (carrots, green beans, bell peppers, onions).
    • Boil green peas if using fresh (or defrost if frozen).

  3. Sauté the Liver/Chicken (Optional):

    • Season diced liver with a little salt and curry, then fry until browned.
    • Remove and set aside.

  4. Cook the Rice:

    • In a pot, combine rice, stock (or water + bouillon), curry, thyme, and salt.
    • Cook on medium heat until the rice is fully done but not soggy. Fluff and let cool.

  5. Stir-Fry Method (Best for vibrant, non-soggy fried rice):

    • Heat 1–2 tbsp oil in a wide frying pan or wok.
    • Add onions, sauté briefly, then add vegetables and stir-fry for about 3–5 minutes.
    • Add the pre-cooked rice in small batches and stir-fry thoroughly to combine.
    • Add liver/chicken if using, and mix everything well.
    • Taste and adjust seasoning.

Tip: For a party-style flavor, fry the rice in small batches to avoid steaming it in the pan.



Serving Suggestions:

Serve with:

  • Fried plantain
  • Fried chicken or turkey
  • Moin moin
  • Salad
